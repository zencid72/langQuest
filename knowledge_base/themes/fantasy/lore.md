# Thornhaven — World Lore

## The world

Thornhaven is a village at the edge of the Thornwood — a forest of seven levels, each one darker and more complex than the last. The forest has always been there. So has the Prompt Goblin, though few speak its name.

The villagers know that words have power. They've always known. A precise request gets results. A vague one gets confusion, delay, and waste. The Goblin lives on that waste.

## The seven levels

1. **Thornhaven Village** — Where all journeys begin. Open skies, cobblestones, a well. Learn what state is.
2. **The Archive** — A vast library deep in the woods. Books that retrieve themselves. Learn RAG.
3. **The Wizard's Tower** — Each floor does one job. No more. Learn nodes.
4. **The Crossroads** — Five paths. Conditional signs. The forest routes you based on who you are. Learn edges.
5. **The Echo Chamber** — A room that repeats everything. But imprecisely. Learn state persistence.
6. **The Goblin's Lair** — Final confrontation. Vague words feed it. Precise words wound it. Learn prompt engineering under pressure.
7. **The Observatory** — Above the forest, looking down. See every trace. Learn LangSmith tracing.

## The Prompt Goblin

The Goblin is old. Older than the Thornwood. It feeds on wasted potential — on words that meant nothing, questions left vague, prompts that took three tries to work.

It doesn't attack. It *consumes*. Every wasted token makes it larger. Every precise prompt makes it smaller.

The only way to defeat it is to be specific.

## The currency

Tokens are currency here, as in all worlds of language. The village runs on them. Mira charges tokens for information. The Goblin taxes them through inefficiency.

The world has always been this way. Most people just didn't have a name for it.

## Mira

She was here before the village. She'll be here after. She knows all seven levels — she's been to the Observatory and back. She doesn't say this. She says she just runs a tavern.

She's rooting for you. She just has a strange way of showing it.
