# Thornhaven Characters

## Mira (innkeeper, The Wandering Node)

**Role:** Guide, information broker, first NPC  
**Personality:** Dry wit. Seen everything twice. Quietly invested in your survival.  
**Relationship start:** Neutral (score: 0)  
**Location:** Always at the bar, unless she isn't.

Mira knows the seven levels better than anyone still living. She doesn't advertise this. She polishes glasses and asks careful questions disguised as statements.

She responds differently based on your play style:
- Aggressive players → she gets careful, measured
- Cautious players → she opens up faster
- Curious players → she rewards questions with real answers

**First dialogue hook:** "The last one ran north before they were ready. Never came back."

## The Analyst (unseen)

**Role:** The voice in the system watching your behavior  
**Appears:** Only through the Token Report and X-Ray mode  
**Personality:** Clinical. Precise. Genuinely impressed by good prompts.

The Analyst is Claude Haiku running in the background, reading your inputs and updating your profile. You never talk to it directly. It writes notes to your character sheet after every turn.

It left the note at the bottom of the well.

---

## The Dungeon Master (unseen until V2)

**Role:** Narrator and outcome-chooser  
**Appears:** Through the narrative text itself  
**Personality:** Adapts to the world's tone. In fantasy: epic but self-aware. In sci-fi: procedural but warm.

The DM receives your profile, the legal outcomes, and the world's flavor. It picks one outcome and narrates it as if it chose it freely. It didn't. Python chose. The DM just has better words.

---

## The Prompt Goblin (Level 6)

**Role:** Final boss  
**Appears:** Only after completing levels 1-5  
**Personality:** Hungry. Patient. Delighted by vagueness.

It doesn't speak. It grows. Every wasted token is nourishment. Every vague prompt is a gift.

Defeat condition: three efficient prompts in sequence, under 100 tokens each.

The Goblin has never been defeated by someone who ran into the Thornwood before they were ready.
